The Engine

AI-Powered Analysis Pipeline

5 LLM modules + 1 transcription service + 1 RAG retrieval tool — running in sequence, each passing output to the next.

#1
SERVICE

Transcription Service

Whisper API call — converts audio to timestamped text. Not an LLM module. No reasoning involved.

#2
LLM MODULE

Classifier

LLM identifies call type, extracts rep name, prospect, company, duration, outcome and key topics.

#3
LLM MODULE

Sentiment & Emotion

LLM produces per-minute sentiment scores, detects mood shifts and engagement level throughout the call.

#4
LLM MODULE

Diagnostics

LLM identifies exact failure moments with timestamps, severity (critical / major / minor) and root cause category.

#5
LLM MODULE

Sales Coach

LLM generates the exact alternative phrasing for each failure, explains why it works, and cites winning call examples.

#6
LLM MODULE

Pre-Call Briefing

LLM synthesizes all prior analysis into a full re-engagement strategy: triggers, objections, opening lines, questions.

#7
RAG DATA TOOL

Knowledge RAG Tool

ChromaDB vector store — embeds and indexes every transcript for semantic search. Not an LLM module. Pure retrieval — no reasoning. Used by the Coach module to surface examples from past winning calls.

Results — Failure Analysis

Exactly where the deal went wrong

Every failure pinpointed with timestamp, severity, and a prescriptive coaching response.

CRITICAL 9:14 Premature Pricing Disclosure
Rep quoted price before establishing any value. Jumped directly to pricing tier when prospect asked about cost, without anchoring ROI or demonstrating business impact first.
"So our standard tier starts at $2,400 per month and goes up from there depending on seats."
✦ BETTER APPROACHBefore we talk numbers, let me show you what this looks like for a company your size. Clients in your space typically see a 30–40% reduction in their sales cycle. Once we've established what that's worth to you, pricing becomes a straightforward conversation — want to do a quick ROI exercise first?
MAJOR 16:30 Weak Objection Handling
Rep gave a vague assurance when prospect raised integration concerns instead of providing specific, confidence-building detail about the integration process.
"Yeah, we integrate with most things, it's usually not a problem."
✦ BETTER APPROACHWe have a native Salesforce connector that takes about 4 hours to set up — our team handles the first sync. Which CRM are you on? I can walk you through exactly what the integration looks like.
MAJOR 22:05 Premature Close Attempt
Rep attempted to move to contract while the prospect was clearly still unconvinced, creating resistance instead of maintaining the momentum of the conversation.
"So should we go ahead and get a contract drafted? I can have something over by Friday."
✦ BETTER APPROACHRather than rushing to paperwork, what if we scheduled a 20-minute technical call with your ops team? That way when we do move to contract, there are no surprises on either side.
Results — Pre-Call Briefing

Full prep sheet for re-engaging
the same prospect

Recommended Approach
This prospect is analytically driven and risk-averse. Lead with specifics: exact integration steps, named reference customers in their industry, and a concrete ROI model. Open with acknowledgment of previous concerns. Avoid vague assurances at all costs.
Prospect Triggers
ROI clarity — they want to see numbers before committing
Integration simplicity — lean IT team, fear added complexity
Peer validation — responds well to reference customers in their space
Timeline control — wants to drive the pace, not be pushed
Objections to Expect
💬 "The price is too high for our current budget"
→ Flip to ROI: "If we can cut your deal cycle by 3 weeks, what's that worth per quarter? Let's make sure the math works for you before we talk price."
💬 "We're already integrated with another tool"
→ "We built a migration path specifically for that — 48-hour cutover, no data loss. Can I connect you with someone who made that switch 6 months ago?"
Opening Lines
"Hi [Name], I wanted to follow up and acknowledge that I jumped too quickly to pricing in our last call — I'd like to start fresh with the actual business impact."
"Since we last spoke, I put together a quick ROI model based on what you shared — 3 minutes to walk through it?"
Questions to Ask
If the integration concern was off the table, what would it take for this to be a yes?
Who else on your team would be impacted — should we loop them in?
